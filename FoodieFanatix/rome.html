Ristorante Romano

Nestled in the heart of Rome’s historic center, Ristorante Romano exudes charm and authenticity, making it a standout in the city’s culinary scene. Stepping inside, you're greeted with warm, rustic decor that sets the stage for a memorable dining experience. I started with their signature antipasto platter, featuring a delightful assortment of cured meats, cheeses, and marinated vegetables sourced locally. Each bite was a perfect balance of flavors, showcasing the freshness of the ingredients. For the primo piatto, I opted for their renowned cacio e pepe. The pasta was cooked to perfection—al dente, coated in a creamy pecorino Romano sauce with a hint of freshly ground black pepper. It was a true homage to Roman culinary tradition, simple yet divine. Moving on to the secondo, the saltimbocca alla Romana stole the show. Tender veal, prosciutto, and sage cooked in a white wine sauce melted in my mouth, accompanied by crispy roasted potatoes. To end on a sweet note, I indulged in their tiramisu, which was nothing short of spectacular. Layered with mascarpone cream and soaked in espresso, each spoonful was a taste of heaven. The attentive service and cozy ambiance further enhanced my dining experience at Ristorante Romano. For anyone seeking authentic Roman cuisine with impeccable quality, this restaurant is a must-visit.

Trattoria del Mondo

Trattoria del Mondo, located off a quiet alley in Rome, promised a taste of traditional Italian cuisine but fell short of expectations. Despite its charming exterior and cozy ambiance, the dining experience left much to be desired. Starting with the antipasto, the selection was uninspired and lacked the freshness I had hoped for. The bruschetta tasted stale, and the caprese salad was underwhelming, with flavorless tomatoes. Moving on to the pasta course, I ordered their carbonara, a classic Roman dish known for its creamy sauce and perfectly cooked guanciale. Unfortunately, what arrived was overly salty and drowned in a heavy, curdled sauce, a far cry from the authentic carbonara I expected. The main course, a seafood risotto, was disappointing as well. The rice was overcooked, and the seafood tasted bland and slightly undercooked. Service was slow and inattentive, with long waits between courses despite the restaurant not being busy. The overall dining experience at Trattoria del Mondo was disappointing, failing to deliver on the promise of authentic Italian flavors. For those seeking a true taste of Rome’s culinary delights, I would recommend exploring other options in the city.

Osteria Moderna

Located in a trendy neighborhood of Rome, Osteria Moderna aims to blend traditional Italian cuisine with a contemporary twist. While the concept is intriguing, the execution left me feeling underwhelmed. The restaurant boasts a sleek, minimalist decor and a lively atmosphere, but the food failed to impress. Starting with the appetizer, the burrata salad lacked the freshness I expected. The burrata itself was creamy but lacked the rich, buttery flavor characteristic of high-quality cheese. Moving on to the main course, I opted for the lasagna al forno, a dish I was eagerly anticipating. Unfortunately, the lasagna lacked depth of flavor, with a tomato sauce that tasted overly acidic and a meat filling that was sparse. Service was efficient but lacked warmth and attention to detail. The wine selection was good, offering a variety of local and international options. Despite its modern approach, Osteria Moderna did not succeed in delivering a memorable dining experience. While it may appeal to those looking for a trendy atmosphere, diners seeking authentic Italian flavors may leave feeling unsatisfied.
